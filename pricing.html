Frequently asked questions

Are there any hidden fees?
No. Worldline charges only the transaction fees listed on this page. There are no setup fees, no monthly platform fees on the standard plan, and no contract lock-in. The only additional charges that may apply are chargebacks (€30 each), refund processing (€0.05), and currency conversion (1.50%) — all clearly stated in our rate card above.
How long does settlement take?
Standard settlement is next business day for card payments processed before the daily cut-off time. SEPA Direct Debit typically settles within 2–3 business days. Payout timelines may vary depending on your country and risk profile — enterprise merchants can negotiate same-day settlement as part of a custom agreement.
Do I need to sign a long-term contract?
No. The standard pay-as-you-go pricing has no minimum term or lock-in. You can start and stop at any time. Enterprise and volume agreements may have a defined term for the agreed rates, but these are always clearly outlined before you sign.
What happens if a customer disputes a payment?
If a cardholder disputes a transaction (chargeback), Worldline will notify you immediately and guide you through the response process. A €30 chargeback fee applies per dispute. If you win the dispute, the original transaction amount is returned to your balance. Strong evidence and fast responses are the most effective way to contest fraudulent chargebacks.
Can I accept payments in multiple currencies?
Yes. Worldline supports payments in 150+ currencies. When processing in a currency other than your primary settlement currency, a 1.50% conversion fee applies. Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC) is also available on select hardware terminals, allowing international customers to pay in their home currency at point of sale.
What is IC+ pricing?
IC+ (interchange-plus) pricing passes the actual interchange fees set by card networks directly to you, plus a fixed Worldline margin. This is typically more cost-effective for high-volume merchants because you benefit directly when customers pay with lower-cost cards. It is available on request for eligible enterprise accounts.
