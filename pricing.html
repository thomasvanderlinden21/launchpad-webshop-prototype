Frequently asked questions

What does the €0.15 minimum transaction cost mean?
Every transaction has a minimum processing cost of €0.15, regardless of the transaction value or payment method. This means that even if the calculated percentage fee is lower than €0.15, you will be charged €0.15. For domestic debit transactions with a flat fee of €0.13, the minimum of €0.15 applies instead.
When does the commercial cards surcharge apply?
The +1.20% commercial cards surcharge applies whenever a business or corporate card is used instead of a consumer card — on Visa, Mastercard, Maestro, Visa Debit, Debit Mastercard, and Bancontact. This is determined automatically by the card network and added on top of the basic rate. You cannot predict which card your customer will present, so it's worth factoring this into your margin for B2B transactions.
What triggers the non-EEA surcharge?
The +1.20% non-EEA surcharge applies when a card issued outside the European Economic Area is used for a Visa, Mastercard, Maestro, Visa Debit, or Debit Mastercard transaction. For example, a tourist paying with a US-issued Visa card would trigger this surcharge. It does not apply to Alipay+, WeChat Pay, UnionPay, JCB, or Diners/Discover, which are priced at a flat 1.40% for all regions.
What happens when a customer disputes a payment?
If a cardholder disputes a transaction, a chargeback fee of €30.00 applies regardless of the outcome. Worldline will notify you and guide you through submitting evidence. If you win the dispute, the original transaction amount is returned to your balance — but the €30.00 fee is not refunded. Maintaining clear transaction records and delivery evidence is the best way to contest chargebacks successfully.
What is the compliance guarantee fee?
The €60.00/year compliance guarantee covers PCI DSS file management — the ongoing administration to keep your merchant account compliant with card network security standards. This is charged once per merchant, not per terminal. So whether you have one device or five, you only pay this fee once per year.
Are refunds charged the same rate as the original payment?
Yes. Refunds are processed at the same rate as the original transaction. If the original payment was a Visa Debit domestic transaction at €0.13, the refund is also €0.13. For percentage-based transactions, the refund fee is calculated on the refunded amount at the original rate.
