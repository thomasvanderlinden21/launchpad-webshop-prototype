Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between in-store and online rates?
In-store (card-present) transactions carry lower rates because the physical card and cardholder are verified at the point of sale, reducing fraud risk. Online (card-not-present) transactions are slightly higher to account for the increased risk of fraud and chargebacks. For example, Visa Credit is 1.40% in-store versus 1.65% online for domestic transactions.
What does the €0.15 minimum transaction cost mean?
Every transaction has a minimum processing cost of €0.15, regardless of the transaction value or payment method. This means that even if the calculated percentage fee is lower than €0.15, you will be charged €0.15. For domestic debit transactions with a flat fee of €0.13, the minimum of €0.15 applies instead.
When does the commercial cards surcharge apply?
The +1.20% commercial cards surcharge applies whenever a business or corporate card is used instead of a consumer card — on Visa, Mastercard, Maestro, Visa Debit, Debit Mastercard, and Bancontact. This applies to both in-store and online transactions. It is determined automatically by the card network and added on top of the basic rate.
What triggers the non-EEA surcharge?
The +1.20% non-EEA surcharge applies when a card issued outside the European Economic Area is used for a Visa, Mastercard, Maestro, Visa Debit, or Debit Mastercard transaction — both in-store and online. For example, a tourist paying with a US-issued Visa card would trigger this surcharge. It does not apply to Alipay+, WeChat Pay, UnionPay, JCB, or Diners/Discover.
Are Apple Pay and Google Pay charged differently?
No. Apple Pay and Google Pay are tokenised versions of underlying cards — the transaction is processed at the same rate as the card stored in the wallet. A Visa Debit card in Apple Pay costs the same as a physical Visa Debit tap at the terminal. No wallet surcharge applies.
What happens when a customer disputes a payment?
If a cardholder disputes a transaction, a chargeback fee of €30.00 applies regardless of the outcome. Worldline will notify you and guide you through submitting evidence. If you win the dispute, the original transaction amount is returned to your balance — but the €30.00 fee is not refunded. Online merchants typically see higher chargeback rates; maintaining clear order records and delivery evidence is key.
What is the compliance guarantee fee?
The €60.00/year compliance guarantee covers PCI DSS file management — the ongoing administration to keep your merchant account compliant with card network security standards. This is charged once per merchant, not per terminal or integration. Whether you have one device, five terminals, or an online store, you pay this fee once per year.
Are refunds charged the same rate as the original payment?
Yes. Refunds are processed at the same rate as the original transaction. If the original payment was a Visa Debit domestic transaction at €0.13, the refund is also €0.13. For percentage-based transactions, the refund fee is calculated on the refunded amount at the original rate.
